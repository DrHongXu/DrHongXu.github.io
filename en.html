➔ Welcome friends from the World!

Energy powers human civilization, and energy materials tackle the challenges of energy security and climate change, catalyzing the radical AI-driven industrial revolution. At the interplay of Innovation–Integration-Intelligence, Dr. Hong Xu is dedicated to advance critical materials for e-power systems and smart grid applications, including solar cells, lithium-ion batteries, electrolyzers, and hydrogen fuel cells. By reforming functional materials and device architectures, his work enhances energy efficiency, materials durability, and the lab-to-market scalability of sustainable energy systems. His efforts have been rewarded with publications and patents (granted/filed) addressing green transition, in collaboration with global institutions. Notably, his systematic work in X-ray tomography for fuel cell studies achieved cutting-edge temporal (0.1 s) and spatial (0.4 μm) resolution worldwide, leveraging joint efforts with Toyota Central R&D Labs for critical materials research, and Swiss Light Source for 4D imaging development. This milestone was highlighted by the Paul Scherrer Institute and presented at ECS in Atlanta as invited talk with travel grant.

A scholar by heart, he earned his PhD in Renewable Energy from ETH Zurich, fully funded by the SNSF. Prior to that, he obtained dual MSc degrees in Materials Science and Physics, jointly awarded by the Technical University of Munich and the University of Rennes, supported by an Erasmus Scholarship. In pursuit of impactful scientific and policy research, he gained practical experience both in academia at European XFEL, University of Munich, and Polytechnic Institute of Paris, and in industry at Toyota, Infineon, and Freudenberg. His academic journey began at Beijing Transport University, where he studied Materials Chemistry with an innovation scholarship, and trained at the Chinese Academy of Sciences.
